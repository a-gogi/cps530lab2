Fantastic Mr. Fox (2009)

★★★★☆
An urbane fox cannot resist returning to his farm raiding ways and then must help his community survive the farmers' retaliation.


This film was one of the few animation films that I could watch many times over and over again and still enjoy it. Instead of calling it a cult film, since it has no cult qualities, it is just a great classic to add to Wes Anderson's library.

I enjoyed that the characters had complex personalities and each were able to learn something from their experiences in the film. We see Mr. Fox struggling with living a quiet domestic life, while Mrs. Fox wanted exactly that. Ash develops a inferiority complex over his cousin Kristopherson, who is too naive to know what he wants to do with his life.

Ex-Machina (2014)

★★★★☆
A young programmer is selected to participate in a ground-breaking experiment in synthetic intelligence by evaluating the human qualities of a breath-taking humanoid A.I.


I'm not a big fan of sci-fi films but this one really was different from the rest. Unlike other sci-fi genre movies, this one chose to focus on one topic and that was artifical intelligence. I really liked that they took such a simple plot, in a short time setting, and made it into so much more.

Filled with a smashing plot twist, Ex-Machina redefines modern sci-fi to really crawl into the audiences skin and ask the tough questions.
